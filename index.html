Why Local Processing Matters

EditForge is engineered around a privacy-first architecture. Traditional online editors force you to upload sensitive images, documents, and videos to remote servers where they can be scanned, logged, or retained. EditForge runs entirely inside your browser using technologies like HTML5 Canvas, WebAssembly (ffmpeg.wasm), and modern JavaScript APIs. Your files never leave your device, making it ideal for professionals handling confidential media.

Supported formats include JPEG, PNG, WebP, PDF, MP4, MOV, WebM, AVI, MP3, WAV, and AAC. Because all computation happens client-side, you can use EditForge on airplanes, secure internal networks, or any environment without internet—after the initial page load.

Optimized for Speed and Security

Our image engine leverages hardware-accelerated canvas rendering. The video pipeline uses ffmpeg.wasm (FFmpeg compiled to WebAssembly) for frame-accurate transcoding directly in memory. PDF optimization uses pdf-lib to rewrite document structures, strip unnecessary metadata, and reduce bloat without requiring a remote API. Whether you are optimizing a portfolio, compressing a meeting recording, or stripping location data from a photo, EditForge gives you complete control.

Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 2026

EditForge is committed to absolute privacy. Our application is designed as a fully client-side tool, which means all file processing occurs locally on your device using your browser's resources.

No Data Collection

We do not operate a backend server for file processing. Images, PDFs, and videos uploaded into the application are handled entirely within your browser's memory via HTML5 Canvas, WebAssembly, and JavaScript APIs. We do not collect, store, transmit, or share your files or metadata.
